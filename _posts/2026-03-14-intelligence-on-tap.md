A lot has been written on this topic by people who are much smarter than me, but this really feels like the new leverage. Anything that can happen digitally can be created pretty quickly.

We are right now living in a world where humans and AI agents that live on the internet can reason. When I talk about AI agents, I'm only talking about the ones living on the internet right now that can reason — not physical robots or world models or anything of that sort.

Anything that can be created digitally can be created at will, at ease, using these agents. Anyone who can leverage how best to use this is only going to do and create a lot more with their help.

People have said this a lot and it sort of seems a bit of a cliche now, but intelligence is really available on tap and you can just do wonders with it. Not many people have realised this first-hand, and not everyone who has experienced it first-hand is going to win this game. The old rules still apply:

- You need to be consistent.
- You need to know how to best use the leverage.
- You need to know yourself.
- You need to know what you need to be known for.
- You need to know what your skill sets are.

These rules are all very timeless. It feels like a bit of an intersection: you have a set of old rules for success, and then the new game is to leverage agents.

To be honest, every minute that I don't spend time with agents actually feels very less productive. That does not mean that things like relationship building, talking with people, working with people suddenly vanish — but the things you can create digitally for every individual are just massive now. There is a whole lot of digital leverage available for humans, at least right now, because of the combination of humans and agents.

We are in this phase where humans are leveraging agents, but there is soon going to be a future where agents will be leveraging humans. Until then, let's make the best use of the agents and scale our potential and impact in this world.

The amount of words I type has considerably reduced with tools like [Wispr Flow](https://www.wispr.com). Manually writing code is virtually zero now — virtually non-existent — but I am shipping a lot more code than I've shipped in the past. I believe I understand systems thinking and that is helping me leverage the most out of AI agents.

Onwards and upwards.
